Square Defence Chess

Rules:

  1. The goal is to control and guard a randomly chosen empty square on the chessboard.
  2. Begin with a standard chessboard. Randomly select an empty square; this square will be the focus of the game.
  3. Players are not allowed to move any of their pieces onto the chosen square.
  4. Players take turns making standard chess moves, with the primary objective of controlling and guarding the chosen square.
  5. The game ends when a player cannot make a legal move that would add a piece to the board that guards the chosen square.
  6. The player with more pieces guarding the square at the end of the game is the winner.
